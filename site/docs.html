Getting Started

Welcome to SYNTH3D. This guide will get you from zero to your first generated 3D world in under 5 minutes.

Installation

SYNTH3D is a standalone desktop application. Download the installer for your platform from the Download page and follow the steps below.

ℹ
Unity is required. SYNTH3D uses Unity as its rendering engine. Make sure you have Unity 2022.3 LTS or later installed before running SYNTH3D.
  1. Download the installer for your OS (Windows / macOS)
  2. Run the installer and follow on-screen instructions
  3. On first launch, SYNTH3D will detect your Unity installation automatically
  4. You're ready to generate your first scene

Quick start

Once installed, open SYNTH3D and type your first prompt into the input bar at the top of the window.

Example prompt 
"A mossy stone temple in a rainforest, shafts of light through the canopy, butterflies"

Press Enter or click Generate. SYNTH3D will process the prompt and render the scene in Unity. Generation typically takes 2–6 seconds depending on scene complexity.

Prompt basics

Good prompts are descriptive and specific. Think of yourself as directing a film — you're setting the scene, the mood, the time of day, and the atmosphere.

What to include

  • Setting — Where is the scene? (forest, city, space, underwater)
  • Time of day / weather — dawn, midday, dusk, midnight, rainy, foggy
  • Mood / atmosphere — eerie, peaceful, dramatic, desolate
  • Key objects — what should be in the scene? (ruins, trees, a bridge)
  • Lighting — warm golden light, cool moonlight, harsh noon sun

FAQ

Is SYNTH3D free?

The beta is completely free. Future pricing will be announced before public launch.

Do I need coding knowledge?

No. SYNTH3D is a graphical application designed for creators of all backgrounds. You only need to write prompts in plain language.

Can I use generated scenes commercially?

Yes. Scenes you generate with SYNTH3D are yours to use however you like, including in commercial projects.

Does it work offline?

Scene generation requires an internet connection for the AI model. Rendering and export happen locally.
